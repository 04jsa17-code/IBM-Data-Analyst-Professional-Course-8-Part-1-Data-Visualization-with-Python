# IBM Data Analyst Professional Certificate – Course 8 Part 1: Data Visualization with Python

**Project Title:** Automobile Sales Analysis During Recession Periods – Data Visualization

This is Part 1 of the final project for **Course 8** of the IBM Data Analyst Professional Certificate on Coursera.

## Overview

- Analyzed historical automobile sales data to understand the impact of recession periods on vehicle sales
- Developed multiple visualizations using **Matplotlib**, **Seaborn**, and **Folium** to uncover trends and patterns
- Examined relationships between automobile sales and key economic indicators such as GDP, unemployment rate, consumer confidence, and advertising expenditure
- Compared sales performance across different vehicle types during recession and non-recession periods

## Technologies Used

- Python
- Jupyter Notebook
- pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Folium

## Visualizations Created

- Line charts showing average automobile sales fluctuation year over year
- Seaborn bar charts comparing sales trends by vehicle type during recession vs non-recession periods
- GDP variation subplots comparing recession and non-recession periods
- Bubble plot displaying the impact of seasonality on automobile sales
- Scatter plots identifying relationships between consumer confidence, vehicle price, and automobile sales
- Pie charts showing advertising expenditure distribution during recession and non-recession periods
- Line plot analyzing the effect of unemployment rate on vehicle type and sales

## Key Insights

- Automobile sales drop significantly during recession periods, with sales nearly 3x higher during non-recession periods
- Executive and sports cars experience the steepest declines during recessions while SuperMiniCars are the most resilient
- Advertising expenditure remains relatively stable compared to the volatility of automobile sales
- Rising unemployment rates during recessions correlate strongly with declining automobile sales across all vehicle types
